Ethiopian Pharmaceutical Supply Service (EPSS) Dessie Branch is a government pharmaceutical supply institution committed to ensuring the continuous availability of quality, safe, and affordable medicines and medical supplies.


Who We Are

EPSS Dessie Branch serves public health facilities, hospitals, and other healthcare institutions by efficiently managing procurement, storage, and distribution of pharmaceuticals in accordance with national standards.

Our Vision

To be a leading pharmaceutical supply center that supports equitable access to essential medicines and improved healthcare outcomes.

Our Mission

  • Ensure uninterrupted supply of essential medicines
  • Maintain quality, safety, and affordability
  • Support public health programs and facilities
  • Strengthen pharmaceutical supply chain management

About Ethiopian Pharmaceuticals Supply Service

Ethiopian Pharmaceuticals Supply Service (EPSS) Dessie Branch

Remains a critical strategic hub within the Amhara Region. It is part of the northwestern cluster and serves as one of the four primary hubs supplying the region's vast network of public health facilities.

Service Coverage & Distribution



  • Facility Reach:

    • The Dessie hub contributes to the service of over 5,000 public health facilities across Ethiopia, specifically managing a catchment area that includes zones in eastern and southern Amhara.
  • Regional Role:

    • It operates in tandem with the Bahir Dar, Gondar, and Debre Markos hubs to cover the Amhara National Regional State’s approximately 87 public hospitals and 873 public health centers.
  • Operational Stability:
    •
    After a significant reopening in 2022 following regional instability, the branch was restocked with an initial 190 million Birr worth of essential medicines to restore service continuity. Logistics and Quality Assurance
  • Integrated Logistics:

    • The branch utilizes the Integrated Pharmaceutical Logistics System (IPLS) to manage the reporting and requisition of health commodities, ensuring data-driven supply decisions.
  • Inventory Modernization:

    • Dessie is part of the national shift toward improved warehousing standards. National statistics for 2026 show that pharmaceutical wastage has been reduced to under 0.8% due to better tracking and storage protocols implemented at regional branches like Dessie.
  • Supply Volume:

    • The branch handles a diverse portfolio including revolving drug fund (RDF) products, specialized laboratory reagents, and essential program drugs (e.g., vaccines and oncology supplies).

  • Strategic Importance


    • The Dessie Branch is vital for reaching remote communities in the region that are far from the central Addis Ababa warehouses. By maintaining a decentralized storage and distribution point, EPSS minimizes delivery lead times and ensures that life-saving pharmaceuticals are available at the point of care even in difficult-to-reach areas.
