EPSA Dessie Branch Operations

The EPSA Dessie Branch Operations dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of daily activities, resource management, and performance metrics. It allows staff and management to track the progress of ongoing programs and ensure smooth branch operations.

This dashboard highlights critical information such as inventory levels, staff assignments, distribution status, and reports on branch services. Users can quickly access essential data to make informed decisions and improve operational efficiency.

With a focus on transparency and accountability, the EPSA Dessie Branch Operations dashboard serves as a central hub for monitoring, reporting, and planning. It ensures that the branch meets its goals while maintaining high standards of service delivery to the community.

⚠️ The Ethiopian Pharmaceutical Supply Service (EPSA) Portal serves

The Ethiopian Pharmaceutical Supply Service (EPSA) has activated its Emergency Response Protocol due to detected critical shortages in essential medications across multiple healthcare facilities. Immediate intervention is required to prevent potential treatment disruptions affecting approximately 15 regional hospitals and 42 health centers. Current stock analysis reveals that vital antibiotics, cardiovascular medications, and insulin supplies have fallen below the 10% safety threshold, with complete depletion projected within 72 hours if supply chains are not immediately reinforced. All regional warehouses have been placed on high alert status, and contingency redistribution measures are being implemented under Article 7 of the National Pharmaceutical Emergency Act.



⚠️ Key Portal Features:


  • IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY PROCUREMENT - Authorize emergency funding for urgent medication imports
  • REDISTRIBUTION MANDATE - Execute compulsory stock transfer from overstocked facilities
  • 24/7 OPERATIONS ACTIVATION - Implement round-the-clock warehouse and delivery operations
  • HEALTHCARE FACILITY NOTIFICATION - Issue official alerts to all affected medical institutions
  • SUPPLIER ESCALATION - Activate emergency contracts with pre-approved pharmaceutical suppliers
  • QUALITY CONTROL BYPASS - Implement expedited testing protocols for critical medications
  • TRANSPORTATION COMMANDEERING - Secure priority transportation routes and vehicles
  • PATIENT SAFETY PROTOCOLS - Deploy alternative treatment guidelines where supplies are limited
